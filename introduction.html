Introduction

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is a cornerstone course in the computer science undergraduate curriculum. DAA equips students with systematic techniques to design efficient algorithms and analyze their correctness, performance, and limitations. As computing systems become more complex and data-intensive, the ability to design algorithms that are both correct and efficient is more critical than ever. From web search engines and social media platforms to network routing and cryptography, efficient algorithms form the backbone of modern computing applications. While the theoretical understanding of algorithms is crucial, students often face challenges when it comes to: Translating algorithmic ideas into code, Understanding trade-offs between time and space complexities, Choosing the right algorithmic paradigm (like divide and conquer, greedy, dynamic programming), and Reasoning about algorithmic efficiency on real-world inputs. Through a series of structured and interactive experiments, students gain hands-on experience in: Implementing classic and advanced algorithms, Experimenting with algorithmic design paradigms, Analyzing the time and space complexity of solutions, Comparing different algorithms for the same problem, and Developing an intuition for algorithmic efficiency and problem-solving. This lab helps students not only understand how algorithms work, but also why one algorithm may outperform another in different scenarios. The aim is to build deep algorithmic thinking — a skill essential for competitive programming, system design, research, and real-world software development.
